European Earwigs


European Earwigs are reddish brown in color, with a flattened and elongate body, and slender, beaded antennae. They are about 12–15 mm long. Spend the day time in cool, dark, inaccessible places such as flowers, fruits, and wood crevices. Active primarily at night, they seek out food ranging from plant matter to small insects. They have well-developed wings but rarely used. They are commonly seen in the gardens.

Damages


Cause significant damage to crops, flowers, and fruit orchards when at high numbers. It eats almost any plant materials. Vegetables it feeds upon include cabbage, cauliflower, chard, celery, lettuce, potato, beet, and cucumber among others


Control and Preventions


Keep the garden clean, remove any debris to reduce the available refuge. Set up a trap by placing dampen paper in a pot turned upside down. Earwigs will crawl into the pot. Then kill them. It is said that Earwigs are attracted by Beer but they die once they taste it. Pour some beer in small jars and lay the jars in the garden. Crumpled up newspapers to trap earwigs. Collect them first thing in the morning and knock them into a bucket of soapy water.
